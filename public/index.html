Revision methods — ranked
🧠
Active recall (blank page)
Close everything. Write the full solution from memory — data structures, edge cases, complexity. Only then compare.
Best method
⏱
Timed re-solve
Set a timer (15 min medium, 25 min hard). Solve completely, no hints. If you go over, mark as hard — review sooner.
For mocks
💬
Rubber duck explain
Speak the solution out loud as if teaching it. Explain why each step works. Gaps in explanation = gaps in understanding.
Pattern depth
🔀
Variant drilling
After solving, mutate the problem: change constraints, flip min/max, add a dimension. You retain the pattern, not just the solution.
Hard tier prep
📖
Re-reading your solution
Feels productive. Your brain pattern-matches and says "yes I know this." Mostly wasted time — recognition, not recall.
Avoid
📺
Rewatching video solutions
Only useful the first time you see a pattern. Re-watching Striver/Neetcode for solved problems is passive recognition.
Avoid for revision

Golden rules
1
The 30-minute ruleSpend 30 min genuinely stuck before looking at any hint. That discomfort is where retrieval pathways form.
2
Write your "why", not just the "what""This is sliding window because… the invariant I maintain is…". Forces pattern abstraction, which transfers to new problems.
3
Group reviews by pattern, not by dateAll two-pointer together, then all DP knapsack. Brain builds abstraction, not individual solution memory.
4
Never review the same day you solvedSame-day review is re-reading in disguise — solution is still in working memory. Minimum gap: 1 sleep cycle.
5
The 3-second testWithin 3 seconds of seeing a title, name: the pattern, key data structure, time complexity. If you can't — it belongs in R1.
